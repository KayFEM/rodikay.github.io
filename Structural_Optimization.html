Topological optimization of a roller skate
Lets consider a rectangular beam, like the Figure below, with the material properties of a high strength polymer, with
an applied load resembling a human body in motion and collision. The beam will uphold the forces when doing a
linear elastic analysis, however, there might be an excessive amount of material that will increase cost, weight, and
performance of the structure.
The purpose will be to remove excessive material to optimize the solution and save material, with the help of doing
a linear elastic analysis, by only considering the J2-stresses. Since the von Mises is independent of the hydrostatic
stresses and is purely describing the amount of distortion energy. Therefore, it is a strong tool to determine the yielding
and how to reshape and optimize the structure.
With these material properties, according to Table 1, the solution must satisfy the maximum limit of 20MPa of J2-
stresses, since that is the elastic limit.

The blue areas can be removed, and the simulation can be run again until a satisfactory result in simulated. After several
iterations of removing material, the rectangular beam will result in a shape that resembles an inline skate instead.
However, there are singularities at the supports, with extreme reactions forces that will result in a fault visualization of
the J2 stresses at those areas. Thus, these areas can be neglected in the analysis, and the remaining geometry should be
regarded and optimized.
As the von Mises stress predicts the yielding of the material when the distortion reaches the maximum limit, it can
therefore be used for ductile materials, like the material used for the inline skates. However, von Mises stresses are
based upon considering differences in the principal stresses, and the equation is quadratic, meaning it is always positive,
it can therefore not be representative in determining tensile and compression stresses, which stands paramount for
design criterion. Also, there are areas that still are far away from the elastic limit, but they serve as tensile rods for
structural optimization, which must be considered and not removed only due to the J2-stresses.
