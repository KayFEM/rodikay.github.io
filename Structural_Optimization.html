Topological optimization of a roller skate
Lets consider a beam, with the material properties of a high strength polymer, with an applied load resembling a
human body in motion and collision. The purpose will be to remove excessive material to optimize the solution and
save material, with the help of doing a linear elastic analysis, by only considering the J2-stresses. Since the von Mises
is independent of the hydrostatic stresses and is purely describing the amount of distortion energy. Therefore, it is a
strong tool to determine the yielding and how to reshape and optimize the structure.
With these material properties, see Table 1, the solution must satisfy the maximum limit of 20MPa of J2-stresses.

However, there are singularities at the supports, with extreme reactions forces that will result in a fault visualization of
the J2 stresses at those areas. Thus, these areas can be neglected in the analysis, and the remaining geometry should be
regarded and optimized.
As the deviatoric stress is the difference between the hydrostatic and the principal. Thus, this will be used to discuss
the body distortion in the material. Moreover, as the von Mises stress predicts the yielding of the material when the
distortion reaches the maximum limit, it can therefore be used for ductile materials, like the material used for the inline
skates.
However, von Mises stresses are based upon considering differences in the principal stresses, and the equation is
quadratic, meaning it is always positive, it can therefore not be representative in determining tensile and compression
stresses, which stands paramount for design criterion.
